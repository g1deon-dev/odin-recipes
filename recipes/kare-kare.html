Kare-Kare Recipe

Home

Description

Kare-kare is a traditional Filipino stew characterized by its thick, savory peanut sauce and tender cuts of meat, most commonly oxtail, beef tripe, and beef shank. The meats are boiled slowly until soft, then combined with a rich broth thickened with ground toasted rice and crushed peanuts or peanut butter. A variety of vegetables are incorporated into the dish, typically including banana blossoms (puso ng saging), eggplant, string beans (sitaw), and bok choy (pechay ). To achieve its signature vibrant orange-yellow color, the sauce is infused with annatto seeds (atsuete), which tint the dish without altering the savory flavor profile.

Because the peanut stew is intentionally prepared with a mild and earthy taste, kare-kare is traditionally served alongside bagoong alamang (sautéed fermented shrimp paste). Diners combine a small amount of this salty, umami-rich condiment with each bite over steaming white rice, which balances out the thick, creamy texture of the peanut sauce. Due to the lengthy process required to tenderize the meats and prepare the sauce from scratch, kare-kare is frequently reserved for special occasions, Sunday family lunches, and town fiestas across the Philippines.

Ingredients

  • Meat:

    The type of meat and cut is optional, you could choose from the following:

    • Beef
    • Pork

  • Peanut Sauce & Broth:

    • Peanut Butter: 1 cup plain, unsweetened creamy peanut butter (or finely ground roasted peanuts).
    • Toasted Rice Flour: 1/2 cup ground toasted glutinous or white rice (used to thicken the sauce).
    • Annatto Seeds(Atsuete): 2 tablespoons soaked in 1/2 cup warm water or warm cooking oil (strained to extract the red-orange coloring).
    • Beef Broth: 4 to 6 cups of the reserved liquid from boiling the oxtail and tripe.

  • Vegetables:

    • Banana Blossom (Puso ng Saging):1 small bud, quartered or sliced (soaked in salted water to prevent browning).
    • Eggplants(Talong):2 medium pieces, sliced diagonally into 2-inch pieces.
    • String Beans(Sitaw):1 bundle (about 15–20 stalks), cut into 2-inch lengths.
    • Bok Choy(Pechay)1 to 2 bundles, ends trimmed and leaves separated.

  • Aromatics & Seasonings:

    • Garlic:5 cloves, minced.
    • Onion:1 medium piece, chopped.
    • Cooking Oil:2 to 3 tablespoons (for sautéing aromatics and frying vegetables).
    • Salt:1 to 2 teaspoons (used during meat tenderizing and taste adjustment).
    • Whole Black Peppercorns:1 teaspoon (added when boiling the meat).

  • Essential Condiment:

    • Sautéed Fermented Shrimp Paste(Bagoong Alamang)Served on the side to provide the necessary saltiness that balances the mild, savory peanut sauce.

Steps

  1. Tenderize the meat:Boil the oxtail, tripe, and beef in water with salt and peppercorns for 2 to 3 hours (or pressure cook for 45 minutes) until fork-tender. Drain the meat and reserve 4 to 6 cups of the broth.
  2. Cook the vegetables:Blanch the string beans, eggplant, banana blossom, and bok choy in boiling water or a little broth for 2 to 3 minutes until tender-crisp. Drain and set aside to prevent overcooking.
  3. Sauté the base:In a large pot, heat oil and sauté the minced garlic and chopped onions until translucent and fragrant. Add the cooked meats and stir-fry for a few minutes.
  4. Build the sauce:Pour in the reserved beef broth, peanut butter, toasted rice flour, and strained annatto water. Stir continuously over medium heat and simmer for about 10 minutes until the sauce is smooth and thick.
  5. Assemble and serve:Arrange the cooked vegetables on top of the meat and peanut sauce (or fold them in gently). Serve hot with steaming white rice and sautéed bagoong alamang on the side.
