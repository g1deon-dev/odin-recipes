Description

Sisigis a traditional Filipino pork dish originating from Angeles City, Pampanga. Authentically prepared using boiled, broiled, and grilled parts of a pig's head—specifically the ears, cheeks (maskara), and snout—combined with chicken or pork liver, the dish is recognized for its distinct combination of textures. The cartilage of the pig's ears provides crunch, the cheek meat adds chewiness, and the grilled liver contributes a rich, creamy consistency to the overall mixture.

The flavor profile of sisig centers on a balance of savory, sour, and spicy elements, achieved through generous additions of fresh calamansi juice, chopped onions, and native chili peppers (siling labuyo). While traditional Kapampangan purists prepare the dish without mayonnaise or raw eggs, contemporary variations served across the Philippines frequently incorporate these ingredients for added richness and present the dish sizzling hot on a cast-iron plate. Sisig is widely consumed both as an ulam (main entrée) paired with white rice and as pulutan (bar food served alongside alcoholic beverages).

Ingredients

  • Meat's Base

    • Pig's Ears, Snout, and Pork Belly(Maskara at Liempo)1 kg total weight
    • Pork or Chicken Liver:250g

  • Aromatics & Seasonings:

    • Onions:2 large pieces, finely minced
    • Bird's Eye Chilies (Siling Labuyo):4 to 6 pieces, finely chopped (adjust for spice tolerance)
    • Long Green Chilies (Siling Haba):3 pieces, sliced diagonally
    • Calamansi Juice:1/4 cup freshly squeezed
    • Soy Sauce or Liquid Seasoning:2 to 3 tablespoons
    • Whole Black Peppercorns:1 teaspoon (used for boiling)
    • Dried Bay Leaves:2 to 3 pieces (used for boiling)
    • Salt and Ground Black Pepper:To taste

  • Optional Modern Additions:

    • Mayonnaise:1/4 cup (for binding and extra creaminess)
    • Butter or Margarine:2 tablespoons (for greasing the sizzling plate)
    • Raw Egg:1 piece (cracked over the hot dish right before serving)

Steps

  1. Boil the pork:Place the pig's ears, snout, and belly in a large pot with water, bay leaves, whole peppercorns, and salt. Simmer over medium heat for 45 to 60 minutes until tender, then drain thoroughly and let the meat dry.
  2. Grill the meat:Grill the boiled pork cuts and the raw liver over hot charcoal (or broil in an oven/air fryer) until the exteriors develop a slight char and crispness.
  3. Chop the ingredients:Finely chop the grilled pork, cartilage, and liver into uniform, bite-sized pieces.
  4. Season the mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped meats with the minced onions, chilies, calamansi juice, soy sauce, salt, and ground black pepper. Stir well to evenly distribute the acidity and heat. (Note: If opting for the modern variation, fold in the mayonnaise during this step).
  5. Sizzle and serve: Heat butter or margarine on a cast-iron sizzling plate until smoking hot. Transfer the seasoned sisig mixture onto the plate, crack an egg on top if desired, and serve immediately alongside steaming white rice.
